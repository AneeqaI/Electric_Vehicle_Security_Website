Main blog of the day

There are cases of Subaru cars being remotely accessed through unsecure web systems with bugs that successfully penetrate posing as a security flaw. Hackers were able to access years' worth of data from the user's car history. This is a breach of privacy and personal security may be able to put the users in very dangerous situations if these hackers decide to use blackmail or other methods to get what they want. This was an experimentation that was carried out by Sam Curry on a Subaru he gave to his mother.

He was able to, alongside a researcher Shubham Shah online, exploit the web portals and internet connected features of the 2023 Subaru. He found that he was able to remotely honk the horn, unlock the car, start the ignition and terrifyingly enough access more than a year worth of car history even up the current moment with accurate pings. The tracking was extremely detailed and precise to even know what parking spot she was parked in and the addresses of all her friends she visited. They were able to reassign access to the features to any phone or device of their choice. This could be use in millions of ways which could harm and track people without their consent or knowledge and could be weaponised if in the wrong way.

While Subaru moved quickly to patch the weaknesses after being notified, the episode raises unsettling questions about the entire auto industry's attitude toward customer data. It's surprising that such highly sensitive controls were left so exposed, and even more so that workers had access to such widespread tracking without strict oversight.

Neglecting digital security will make electric car theft as dangerous as physical attacks.

Recent news

In the recent months there have been a significant increase in EV vehicles being the target for crimes. A recent representation of this would be EV’s being the victims of charging cable thefts in the UK. This is becoming a significant issue in the west midlands which is the western part of central England and has faced a significant increase in EV related crimes. There is a steadily increasing rate of thefts and similar occurrences taking place in the area as well as around the rest of England.

There are civilians who have been negatively affected by these crimes and have faced situations where they are unable to get to a charger before they run out of battery. They have expected the chargers to be accessible but because of these thefts, are left without and unable to do anything about it, most likely spending hundreds on tow trucks to get their car back home or to a charger that has not been affected by these criminal activities.

As mentioned by Jo Malam; "To get to a charging point and find that there's no head on to charge it would be a massive problem because normally you've not got enough energy to get to the next one."

This shows the growing security issues representing themselves within the EV’s we are living amongst today.
