Latest Security News

In the recent months there have been a significant increase in EV vehicles being the target for crimes. A recent representation of this would be EV’s being the victims of charging cable thefts in the UK. This is becoming a significant issue in the west midlands which is the western part of central England and has faced a significant increase in EV related crimes. There is a steadily increasing rate of thefts and similar occurrences taking place in the area as well as around the rest of England.

There are civilians who have been negatively affected by these crimes and have faced situations where they are unable to get to a charger before they run out of battery. They have expected the chargers to be accessible but because of these thefts, are left without and unable to do anything about it, most likely spending hundreds on tow trucks to get their car back home or to a charger that has not been affected by these criminal activities.

As mentioned by Jo Malam; "To get to a charging point and find that there's no head on to charge it would be a massive problem because normally you've not got enough energy to get to the next one."

This shows the growing security issues representing themselves within the EV’s we are living amongst today.

Stay Safe by Nelly Antouniadou

‘Hyundai Sued Over EV Car Thefts'

Elliot Ingram was shocked when his Hyundai Ioniq 5 was stolen in seconds from outside his UK home, caught on his security camera. Thieves used a £12,000 device resembling a Nintendo console that mimics key signals to unlock and start electric cars. The device, operating in English and Russian, can duplicate a car's signal in 10 seconds to two minutes and store it for repeated use.Hyundai had known about the vulnerability but didn’t inform customers. Ingram said he would've taken extra precautions if warned.

While Hyundai is now seeking solutions, their silence has hurt customer trust. New laws aim to ban the use and sale of such theft devices.

Source:The Guardian
