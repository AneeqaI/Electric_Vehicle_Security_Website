Practical Safety Methods You Can Use Today

Approach #1
  • Keep backup hardware tools in your car at all times.
  • Make sure to update your car's software regularly.
    • Attackers may use tactics like trying to exploit older software with fewer safety protocols.
    • Newer versions of your EV's software will come built-in with higher levels of security.
  • Make sure you don't park in dangerous areas with a lack of proper lighting.
    • Your EV security is important; however, you must also look out for your own safety!

Approach #2
  • Use strong authentication methods for accessing your car's digital systems.
    • Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) if available to secure your car's mobile app and
      keyless entry features.
    • Weak or easily guessable passwords can leave your vehicle vulnerable to hacking or
      unauthorized access.
  • Regularly check for any unusual or unauthorized activity in your EV's apps or systems.
    • Keep an eye on features like remote start, GPS tracking, or door-lock/unlock settings to spot
      any suspicious actions.
    • If you notice anything unusual, immediately contact the manufacturer or dealership for assistance.

Approach #3
  • Ensure physical security measures are in place to protect your EV from theft.
    • Install a steering wheel lock or wheel clamps if you're parking in an area with
      high theft risk.
    • Consider using a GPS tracking device that works even if the car's system is compromised.
  • Secure your charging cable and station when using public charging points.
    • Use a lockable charging cable or take the cable with you to prevent theft during long
      charging sessions.
    • Always ensure your vehicle is locked while charging to prevent unwanted access to your car.

The Pros and Cons of Electric Vehicles

Pros Cons
Reduced Emissions: EVs produce zero tailpipe emissions,
which helps reduce air pollution and greenhouse gases.		 Limited Range: Although EV ranges have improved, they still
tend to be lower than those of gasoline vehicles, especially
on older or less expensive models.
Lower Maintenance: EVs have fewer moving parts than traditional cars, reducing
the need for maintenance like oil changes and lessening wear on components
like the engine and transmission.		 Limited Charging Stations: In some areas, the charging infrastructure
is still developing, which can make long-distance travel more challenging.
Cheaper Fuel: Electricity is generally cheaper than gasoline, and charging
EVs can be more cost-effective, especially if you charge at home.		 Upfront Cost: EVs tend to be more expensive upfront than gasoline vehicles,
though this is slowly changing as battery costs decrease.
Instant Torque: Electric motors provide instant torque, which can result
in faster acceleration and smoother driving.		 Battery Degradation: Over time, EV batteries can degrade, reducing the vehicles
range and efficiency. However, most manufacturers offer warranties to mitigate this.
EVs reduce dependence on oil, especially when charged using renewable energy sources
like solar or wind power.		 Performance in Extreme Weather: Cold weather can reduce an EV's range, as battery efficiency
drops in lower temperatures, which can be an issue in colder climates.
